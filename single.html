Pearson Correlation Analysis

To reduce the effect of outliers, we only keep the data between 5% and 95% thresholds. To evaluate users’ Twitter engagement, we came up with four measuring metrics for each state:

1. the sum of likes per 400 tweet samples;
2. the sum of retweets per 400 tweet samples;
3. the participation rate, which is the sum of all tweets in this state divided by the population of this state, then multiply ten thousand residents;
4. the average number of followers per user.

Test Results for Pearson Correlation Analysis

From the table below we can see that the Pearson correlation coefficients are all close to zero, with p-values larger than 0.05. This again shows that unfortunately there isn’t a significant correlation between Twitter engagement and voting rate.
Pearson Correlation P-value
Likes -0.17 0.25
Retweets -0.03 0.81
Participation rate 0.22 0.13
Followers -0.11 0.46
